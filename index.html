Public Transportation and Rideshare

Chicago features one of the best public transportation systems in the United States. All of the wedding locations are easily accessible to public transportation. The 'L' train, or Elevated Train, covers the entire city and can transport you the entire weekend. Tickets are very cheap, with single rides costing $2.50 per person and day passes costing only $5 for unlimited rides.

The Ambassador Hotel is within a five minute walk from the Clark/Division Red Line Station. St. George Greek Orthodox Church is within a two minute walk from the Diversey Brown Line Station.

Rideshare services are plentiful throughout the city. Memorial Day Weekend is traditionally a very busy time for tourists, so we recommend either booking rides in advance using the reservation feature or to allow plenty of time in case fares are high.

Once you make your way to either the Ambassador or St. Nicholas Greek Orthodox Church, a shuttle will provide transportation for the rest of the evening.

Cookie Table

The Pittsburgh cookie table, a southwestern Pennsylvania Tradition, is said to have started during the Great Depression. Like others worldwide, Pittsburghers struggled. Still life went on, couples married and often celebrated with a reception after the wedding ceremony. However, many Pittsburghers simply could not afford a wedding cake. Instead, as legend goes, the mother of the bride, extended family, and closest friends baked cookies from scratch with recipes passed down through several generations. By the time Pittsburgh recovered from the Great Depression, the cookie table had become a treasured tradition.

Anne and Adam will be having a cookie table at their wedding. Cookies will be prepared by family and friends from scratch and served at the reception and will also be given to guests to take home at the end of the night.

Any guests who would like to participate in preparing cookies for the wedding are more than welcome to! If you would like to bring cookies, please email the mother of the Groom Danielle at danielledrakulic@aol.com and let her know what type and how many cookies you would like to bring.
